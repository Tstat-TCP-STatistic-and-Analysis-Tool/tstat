Tstat optionally produces a "log_periodic_complete" which periodically dumps a subset of the fields of the "log_tcp_complete". For each TCP connection, the metrics reported in the below table are written in the log at the end of each time bin. Statistics are not incremental, thus they are computed on the packets belonging to the given bin. For a complete description of the fields and the definition of a TCP connection, please refer to the "log_tcp_complete" description.

A new time bin begins when more than 1 second elapses from the beginning of the current time bin. When a new time bin begins, the current time bin ends, and the its statistics are dumped in the log. Notice that, as a consequence of the bin logic, the new time bin does not begin exactly 1 second after the beginning of the current bin, but it begins when Tstat observers the first packet for the TCP flow after that moment.
This as two implications: i) bins do not start exactly after N seconds from the flow first packet, ii) bins are not created when no packet is received.
